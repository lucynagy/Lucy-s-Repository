Chapter 1: Causes of Climate Change

Human activities drive climate change primarily through the emission of greenhouse gases like carbon dioxide, which trap the sun's heat in the atmosphere, causing global warming. Key causes include generating power from fossil fuels, manufacturing goods, deforestation, transportation emissions from vehicles and planes, food production processes, energy use in buildings, and overall consumption patterns. These activities collectively speed up the warming of the planet, making significant environmental challenges.
