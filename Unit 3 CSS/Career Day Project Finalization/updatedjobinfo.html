Civil Rights Lawyer

Description

Civil rights lawyers advocate for justice and equality, defending clients whose civil rights have been violated due to factors like race, religion, color, or sexual orientation. They also support special needs students and their parents in cases of neglect or abuse by schools or others. These lawyers handle several responsibilities including legal research, drafting documents, arguing cases in court, and negotiating settlements. They stay informed about evolving local civil rights laws to best represent their clients. When rights are violated, individuals have the option to pursue civil lawsuits, often with the assistance of a civil rights lawyers to achieve the best legal outcomes.

Average Salary

According to O*NET Online, the median annual wage for lawyers, including civil rights lawyers, was $126,930 as of 2020.

Employers

  • Law firms specializing in civil rights law
  • Non-profit organizations focused on civil rights advocacy
  • Government agencies such as the Department of Justice
  • Legal departments of corporations

Detailed Information

Civil Rights Lawyers represent clients in legal proceedings, draw up legal documents, and advise clients on legal matters. They may specialize in a particular area of civil rights law, such as employment discrimination or housing rights.
